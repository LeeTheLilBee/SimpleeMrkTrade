Platform Modes

Modes describe how the platform is behaving in response to broader market conditions. They help explain why the system may become more aggressive, more selective, or more defensive.

Normal Mode

Normal Mode means the system is operating without elevated stress signals. Opportunities can be ranked and processed under standard account and risk rules.

Reduced-Risk Mode

Reduced-Risk Mode means the system is seeing reasons to become more selective. This may happen during elevated drawdown, unstable market conditions, or exposure-related pressure.

In this mode, the platform may trim trade count, slow aggressiveness, or prioritize only the strongest setups.

Defensive Bear Mode

Defensive Bear Mode means the system sees a more hostile or unstable environment and shifts toward protection-first behavior. In this state, weaker setups may be ignored even if they look attractive in isolation.

High Volatility Conditions

High volatility increases opportunity, but it also increases risk. During elevated volatility, the platform becomes more sensitive to quality, stability, and capital protection.

Why Modes Matter

Modes help users understand that a trade idea is never judged in a vacuum. The same setup may be treated differently depending on whether the platform is operating in a stable, defensive, or elevated-risk environment.
