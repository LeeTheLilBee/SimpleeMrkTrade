How To Use This Page

Account Value

Account Value is the platform’s estimate of total account worth at the moment you are viewing it. It combines the account base with the effect of open-position value changes and realized results that have already been recorded.

This matters because it is the clearest high-level view of whether the system is growing, flat, or under pressure.

Use it as a top-level health metric, not a single-trade decision metric.

Cash

Cash is the amount of capital currently not tied up in active positions. It represents what is immediately available before additional controls and limits are applied.

This matters because low cash can prevent new trades even when strong setups appear.

Buying Power

Buying Power is the amount of capital the system can still deploy after applying account constraints. It is more useful than raw cash when thinking about whether a new trade can actually be taken.

Use Buying Power to understand why trades may be skipped even when they rank well.

Open Positions

Open Positions are the trades the system is currently carrying. They are still active and have not yet been closed by a target, stop, or other exit condition.

This matters because the system is always balancing new opportunities against what is already open.

Equity Curve

The Equity Curve is the visual history of account progression over time. It helps users see how stable or unstable system performance has been, rather than focusing on isolated wins or losses.

A smoother curve generally suggests more consistent behavior. A jagged curve may suggest unstable performance or uneven results.

Market Regime

Market Regime describes the broader market environment the system believes it is operating in. Examples include bullish, bearish, defensive, or neutral behavior.

This matters because not all strategies behave the same way in all market states. Regime context influences selection, ranking, and risk behavior.

Volatility

Volatility refers to how aggressively price is moving. Elevated volatility usually means more opportunity, but it also means more risk and wider price movement.

This matters because a setup that looks strong in a calm environment may need tighter filtering in a more unstable one.

Market Mode

Market Mode is the system’s practical operating posture after combining broader regime and internal market conditions. It helps define whether the platform should behave more aggressively, selectively, or defensively.

Think of Mode as the system’s situational stance, not just a label.

Signals

Signals are possible trade opportunities identified by the platform after passing through multiple layers of analysis. They are not guarantees and should be understood as ranked opportunities rather than promises.

The Signals page is best used to see what the system is noticing right now.

Score

Score is a relative strength measure for a setup. Higher scores generally indicate stronger alignment across the platform’s internal conditions.

Score is useful for prioritizing attention, but it should always be read with context such as confidence, market regime, and current account exposure.

Confidence

Confidence reflects how well the setup matches the current environment. It is a measure of alignment and structure, not certainty.

A high-confidence setup may still lose. Confidence helps compare setups against one another, not eliminate risk.

Top Candidates

Top Candidates are the names the system considers most worthy of attention after ranking and filtering. They are not always executed trades. Some remain research-worthy but may fail later account, risk, or liquidity checks.

Use Candidates as a research prioritization tool.

Proof Engine

The Proof Engine is the reporting layer that tracks what the system actually does over time. It includes outcomes such as trade count, win rate, drawdown, and realized results.

This matters because strategy quality is measured over time, not by one trade.

PnL

PnL means profit and loss. Unrealized PnL shows how open positions are currently performing if marked at current prices. Realized PnL reflects positions that have already been closed.

Use unrealized PnL to monitor open exposure and realized PnL to understand completed results.

Premium Analysis

Premium Analysis is the deeper context layer of the platform. It is designed to explain why a setup matters, what context supports it, and how users should think about it without exposing the full proprietary engine.

This section exists to add depth, not just more noise.

Risk Governor

The Risk Governor is a system-level protection layer that limits exposure when conditions become unfavorable. It helps prevent overtrading, protect capital, and keep behavior disciplined.

If a strong setup is blocked, the reason may be risk governance rather than signal weakness.

FAQ

Is this financial advice?

No. SimpleeMrkTrade is a market intelligence and decision-support platform. Users remain responsible for their own trading decisions.

How are signals generated?

Signals are generated through a multi-layer evaluation process involving market structure, context, and internal ranking logic. Specific formulas, weightings, and filters are proprietary.

Why don’t all signals win?

Markets are uncertain by nature. The platform is built for structured consistency over time, not perfect prediction on every setup.

Why do some strong setups get skipped?

A setup can rank well and still be skipped because of buying power, position caps, account exposure, market mode, or other system-level protections.

Why does Premium exist?

Premium exists to provide deeper context while protecting the platform’s internal edge. It gives users better interpretation without giving away the full underlying architecture.
