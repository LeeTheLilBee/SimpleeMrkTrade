Section 1

General Trading Knowledge

These are the core concepts many traders misunderstand. This section explains the market ideas that shape better decision-making.

Market Structure

Markets do not behave the same way all the time. Trend, range, and transition conditions each reward and punish different behaviors.

  • Trend: continuation setups usually work better when direction is established.
  • Range: many breakouts fail because price snaps back into the middle.
  • Transition: unstable structure creates fake moves and messy signals.

Volatility Regimes

Volatility affects how aggressive or defensive a trader should be. It changes risk, speed, and signal reliability.

  • LOW: slower movement, cleaner structure, less explosive follow-through.
  • NORMAL: balanced conditions and more stable signal interpretation.
  • ELEVATED: larger moves, more emotion, faster invalidation risk.

Signal vs Edge

A signal is an event. An edge is a repeatable advantage. Many traders confuse a flashing indicator with a true decision advantage.

  • A signal can be interesting and still be weak.
  • An edge requires context, filtering, and discipline.
  • The same signal can help in one regime and fail in another.

Why Drawdown Matters

Drawdown is not just a number on a chart. It changes behavior, confidence, and future recovery difficulty.

  • Large losses distort later decisions.
  • Recovery math gets harder as losses deepen.
  • Systems that survive usually respect drawdown early.

Position Clustering Risk

Several good trades can become one oversized bet when they all point at the same sector or theme.

  • Correlation hides inside “different” symbols.
  • Sector crowding reduces real diversification.
  • Portfolio risk matters as much as signal quality.

Overtrading

Many accounts do not fail because there were no opportunities. They fail because weak opportunities were treated like necessary action.

  • More trades does not mean more edge.
  • Weak setups dilute strong ones.
  • Discipline often looks like inactivity.
Section 2

Program-Specific Knowledge

This section explains how the platform thinks. It gives users a real understanding of the system without fully exposing proprietary weighting logic.

Signal Engine

The signal engine combines trend, RSI behavior, breakout structure, and momentum context into a unified score. Higher scores mean stronger alignment across multiple factors, not certainty.

Trade Filter Engine

Not every signal deserves deeper attention. The filter engine removes weaker or context-misaligned ideas before they reach premium layers.

Risk Governor

The risk governor can block deployment even when a trade looks strong. That is intentional. The platform protects the account from overtrading, overconcentration, and poor capital behavior.

Research Engine

One of the platform’s strongest advantages is that research keeps running even when live execution is blocked. This keeps edge discovery alive without forcing bad deployment into the simulated account.

Market Regime Engine

The regime engine helps the system decide whether market conditions look bullish, bearish, or unstable. That broader framing changes how signals are interpreted.

Volatility Engine

The volatility engine classifies the market environment and changes system posture. A good setup in normal volatility may not deserve the same treatment in elevated volatility.

Correlation Risk Engine

This engine catches hidden concentration. Several strong ideas in the same sector can quietly become one oversized exposure cluster.

Trade Detail Intelligence

The trade detail layer converts a signal into a readable decision record with thesis, narrative, risk framing, and timeline support.

What the Platform Explains vs What It Protects

The system is designed to be unusually transparent about how it behaves, but not every internal weighting rule is exposed. That balance is intentional. Users get clarity without the architecture losing its value.
